What is a set?

A well defined collection of objects. And, each such object can also be termed as an element of this set.

Okay, but what do you mean by something being "well-defined"?

From what I can gather, if a collection of objects can be described by some objective fact
it's safe to assume that the collection is well-defined and hence it qualifies as a set.

A collection that is based on subjective fact, for e.g., A person's list of favourite movie actors, cannot be considered a set. No matter how much he/she convinces others that it is objective.

In other words, a set is something that is free from all kinds of biases. Something that remains unchanged no matter who decides to sit down and construct it.

Representations

Sets can be represented in two ways:

  • Roster or Tabular form
  • Set-builder form

In the Roster form, elements of the set are enclosed inside curly braces with each of them being separated by commas. Duplicate elements are redundent and therefore not listed.

		For example,
			Let A be the set of natural numbers less than 4,
			then A = {1, 2, 3}
