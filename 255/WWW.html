Motivation?

While working at CERN, Switzerland, Tim Berners-Lee was frustrated at how inefficient and difficult it was to retrieve stored information from the various
computers at the facility. This led him to make a proposal of a system where all the references in a document being associated with the network address of
the thing to which they referred to. This was the idea of a hypertext. Not long after, he invented the World Wide Web in 1989. Then a year later, he invented the first web browser.

Web-spinning Arachnids

A web of what exactly?
The world wide web can be considered as a graph. With information at the nodes and hyperlinks acting as the graphs directed edges, pointing to and
connecting all the mess of both useful and useless media together in an intricate digital cob-web.
